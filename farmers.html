Meet The Farmers

I'm proud to work with local farmers to source our sheep’s milk soap and wool. I believe in the power of local food systems to be stewards of the land and animals and to sustain us in the future.

Maple Hill Farm - Ladysmith, WI

Maple Hill Farm is my source for locally grown sheep’s milk soap. On their farm in Ladysmith, WI Tammy and Brian raise a special type of sheep called East Friesian. This breed originated in northern Germany and Holland. In August 1993, the University of Wisconsin-Madison started a ground-breaking dairy sheep research program and brought the East Friesian breed to the United States. The Spooner Agricultural Research Station was the site of the only dairy sheep research flock in North America, as of 2014. While the dairy sheep program closed in 2016, the bloodlines can be found across the country!

Learn more about Maple Hill and order locally grown sheep’s milk face and body products, meats, cheese, and more!

Windswept Hill Farm - Farmington, MN

Wendy at Windswept Hill Farm raises Leicester Longwools and is my source for wool. Leicester Longwools are among Britain's rarest breeds, categorized as "endangered" by the Rare Breeds Survival Trust, since fewer than 500 registered breeding females remain in the UK and only 750 breeding animals are in the USA. The wool is dense, lustrous and crimps, perfect for handcrafts.

At her farm in Farmington, MN, Wendy shares, "We have two registered rams, including a full brother to last year's national champion. We have five ewes and expect 18 lambs. We also have 20 BlueFaced Leicester ewes and wethers, some crossed with Shetland for diverse fiber length and characteristics. Colors include white, English Blue, and brown." Those are the natural colors of course!

Learn more about Windswept Hill Farm or order yarn, roving, lamb, chicken, and eggs. Set up a visit to the farm or register for a workshop.
